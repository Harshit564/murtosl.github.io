I'm Fine: How to Hit Rock Bottom

I’m fine.

I’m always fine.

I’m not allowed to not be fine.

I have a problem with downplaying.

An Intervention

A Bit of a Headspin

In early May, I went to donate blood for the first time. My friend donated blood every so often and encouraged me to go with her. So we went.

I was felt decent after they were done, so I got up and walked over to the drinks.

Then I started to get really lightheaded, so I sat down at a nearby table.

"Are you OK?"

"I’m fine; I just need to sit down for a bit."

Then I started to get really hot.

"Are you OK?"

"I’m fine."

I thought I'd feel fine in a minute; I didn’t want to bother anyone. This did not fool my friend, who is a nurse.

Everything started to sound funny, and my friend went to get staff members.

I woke up on the floor facing the ceiling, having completely fainted.

I was very not fine there; if my friend wasn’t watching out for me, I could have easily injured myself. Covering up how I was physically feeling did not help anything.

Covering up about how I was feeling was not helping anything.
